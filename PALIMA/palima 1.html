Research Objectives

The general objective of the study is to determine how hydrological variables influence the nest density of janitor fish through a mathematically grounded predictive model. Specifically, the study aims to: Measure and characterize water-flow velocity, depth, and turbulence across selected river segments.
Identify, map, and quantify nest density using GPS-referenced field observations.
Fit the predictive model using ordinary least squares.
Conduct a Monte-Carlo simulation with 10,000 iterations to propagate measurement uncertainty and generate probabilistic nest-density surfaces.
Determine sections of the Marikina River with the highest likelihood of nest concentration based on model outputs.
Provide recommendations for ecological management and riverbank stabilization informed by mathematical predictions.
